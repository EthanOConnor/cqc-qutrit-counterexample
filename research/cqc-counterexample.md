# Exact certificate for a candidate qutrit counterexample to the CQC conjecture

## Status and scope

The finite-dimensional certificate below exactly violates the
complementary-quantum correlation (CQC) inequality proposed by Schneeloch,
Broadbent, and Howell in 2014. The construction uses a state on
$\mathbb C^3\otimes\mathbb C^3$ and the ordinary qutrit computational and
Fourier bases.

The construction, exposition, and initial verification artifacts were
generated by OpenAI GPT-5.6 Pro in a prompted research exchange; the human
repository steward does not claim mathematical authorship or independent
verification. See [`../PROVENANCE.md`](../PROVENANCE.md).

The finite certificate has been checked symbolically and by two structurally
separate numerical implementations generated within that same workflow. A
literature search through July 22, 2026 found no earlier counterexample, and
the most recent directly relevant version-of-record article found still
described the conjecture as open. That search does **not** establish novelty or
priority, and the automated agreement does not constitute independent expert
review. See [cqc-status.md](cqc-status.md) for the source record, proven cases,
related bounds, and search limitations.

## Published conjecture

For a bipartite state $\rho_{AB}$, choose arbitrary rank-one projective
measurements $Z^A,Z^B$, then measurements $X^A,X^B$ such that $X^A$
is mutually unbiased to $Z^A$ and $X^B$ is mutually unbiased to $Z^B$.
The published CQC conjecture asserts

$$
I(Z^A:Z^B)+I(X^A:X^B)\le I(A:B),
$$

where the left side contains classical mutual informations of measurement
outcomes and

$$
I(A:B)=S(A)+S(B)-S(AB)
$$

is quantum mutual information. All logarithms below are base 2. The original
formulation does not require the state or its marginals to have full rank.

Primary source: J. Schneeloch, C. J. Broadbent, and J. C. Howell,
“Uncertainty relation for mutual information,” *Physical Review A* **90**,
062119 (2014),
[doi:10.1103/PhysRevA.90.062119](https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevA.90.062119),
[arXiv:1404.6496](https://arxiv.org/abs/1404.6496).

## State

Inside each qutrit define the logical qubit

$$
|0_L\rangle=|0\rangle,
\qquad
|1_L\rangle=\frac{|1\rangle+|2\rangle}{\sqrt2}.
$$

Define

$$
|\phi\rangle=\frac{|0_L0_L\rangle+|1_L1_L\rangle}{\sqrt2},
$$

$$
|\chi\rangle=
\frac{|0_L0_L\rangle-|1_L1_L\rangle}{\sqrt3}
-\frac{|0_L1_L\rangle+|1_L0_L\rangle}{\sqrt6},
$$

and

$$
\rho=\frac9{10}|\phi\rangle\!\langle\phi|
+\frac1{10}|\chi\rangle\!\langle\chi|.
$$

The two vectors are normalized and orthogonal. In the logical basis, the
coefficient matrix of $|\chi\rangle$ is

$$
A_\chi=
\begin{pmatrix}
1/\sqrt3&-1/\sqrt6\\
-1/\sqrt6&-1/\sqrt3
\end{pmatrix},
\qquad
A_\chi A_\chi^\dagger=\frac12I_2.
$$

Thus both pure states have reduced state $I_2/2$ on the logical support.
Consequently

$$
S(A)=S(B)=1,
\qquad
S(AB)=h_2(1/10),
$$

and

$$
I(A:B)=2-h_2(1/10)=1.5310044064107187.
$$

## Mutually unbiased qutrit measurements

Use the qutrit computational basis $Z$ and Fourier basis $X$:

$$
|x_k\rangle=
\frac{|0\rangle+\omega^k|1\rangle+\omega^{2k}|2\rangle}{\sqrt3},
\qquad
\omega=e^{2\pi i/3}.
$$

They are nondegenerate rank-one projective measurements and are mutually
unbiased in the ambient qutrit Hilbert space because
$|\langle j|x_k\rangle|^2=1/3$ for every $j,k$.

### Why the two measurements see the same logical correlation

On the logical support, the computational measurement has effects

$$
|0_L\rangle\!\langle0_L|,
\quad \frac12|1_L\rangle\!\langle1_L|,
\quad \frac12|1_L\rangle\!\langle1_L|.
$$

The projected Fourier vectors are

$$
\Pi_L|x_0\rangle=|u\rangle
=\frac1{\sqrt3}|0_L\rangle+\sqrt{\frac23}|1_L\rangle,
$$

$$
\Pi_L|x_1\rangle=\Pi_L|x_2\rangle
=\frac1{\sqrt2}|u^\perp\rangle,
\qquad
|u^\perp\rangle=\sqrt{\frac23}|0_L\rangle-\frac1{\sqrt3}|1_L\rangle.
$$

Thus each ambient qutrit measurement restricts to a logical binary projective
measurement with one binary outcome split into two equal physical labels. If

$$
O=
\begin{pmatrix}
1/\sqrt3&\sqrt{2/3}\\
\sqrt{2/3}&-1/\sqrt3
\end{pmatrix},
$$

then $O^TA_\phi O=A_\phi$ and $O^TA_\chi O=-A_\chi$. The two logical
binary measurements therefore have identical joint probabilities for both
pure components, and hence for their mixture.

Direct evaluation gives

$$
P_Z=P_X=
\begin{pmatrix}
29/60&1/120&1/120\\
1/120&29/240&29/240\\
1/120&29/240&29/240
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

The marginal on either side is $(1/2,1/4,1/4)$. Coarse-graining outcomes
1 and 2 gives

$$
\bar P=
\begin{pmatrix}
29/60&1/60\\
1/60&29/60
\end{pmatrix},
$$

a uniform binary symmetric channel with crossover probability $1/30$. The
fine outcomes are conditionally independent local fair-coin refinements, so
they do not alter mutual information. Therefore

$$
I(Z^A:Z^B)=I(X^A:X^B)=1-h_2(1/30)
=0.7891576996814679.
$$

## Exact violation

Hence

$$
\begin{aligned}
\Delta
&=I(Z^A:Z^B)+I(X^A:X^B)-I(A:B)\\
&=h_2(1/10)-2h_2(1/30)\\
&=0.0473109929522169>0.
\end{aligned}
$$

The sign does not depend on floating-point arithmetic:

$$
30\Delta=
\log_2\!\left(\frac{29^{58}}{3^{114}10^{30}}\right),
$$

and the exact integer inequality

$$
29^{58}>3^{114}10^{30}
$$

holds.

## Family and full-rank robustness

For

$$
\rho_\epsilon=(1-\epsilon)|\phi\rangle\!\langle\phi|
+\epsilon|\chi\rangle\!\langle\chi|,
$$

the same calculation gives

$$
I_Z+I_X-I(A:B)=h_2(\epsilon)-2h_2(\epsilon/3).
$$

The choice $\epsilon=1/10$ is a convenient rational point near the maximum.
Numerically, the gap is positive for
$0<\epsilon<0.2258990747\ldots$.

To remove any concern about the rank-two support, define

$$
\rho_{\mathrm{full}}
=\frac{99}{100}\rho+\frac1{100}\frac{I_9}{9}.
$$

Its nine eigenvalues are

$$
\frac{8029}{9000},\quad
\frac{901}{9000},\quad
\underbrace{\frac1{900},\ldots,\frac1{900}}_{7\text{ times}},
$$

and direct recomputation gives

$$
I_Z+I_X-I(A:B)=0.0369567802161937>0.
$$

This sign also has an exact integer certificate. The exact verifier reduces it
to

$$
36000\Delta_{\mathrm{full}}=\log_2(N/D),
$$

where

$$
N=2^{35960}5^{35720}89^{34532}97^{34532}337^{2696}8693^{34772},
$$

$$
D=7^{104356}17^{3604}31^{32116}37^{32116}43^{72240}53^{3604},
$$

and the verifier performs the exact integer comparison $N>D$. Since this
is a full-rank state and the gap is continuous, violations occupy an open
neighborhood in the state space for these fixed measurements.

## Verification

Create an isolated environment and run all three implementations without
Python's `-O` flag, because their certificates use assertions:

```sh
python3 -m venv .venv
.venv/bin/python -m pip install -r requirements.txt
.venv/bin/python scripts/verify_cqc_exact.py
.venv/bin/python scripts/verify_cqc_numpy.py
.venv/bin/python scripts/verify_cqc_mpmath.py
```

- `scripts/verify_cqc_exact.py` recomputes the states, partial traces, ambient
  mutual unbiasedness, the logical-support mechanism, both probability tables,
  both mutual informations, the full-rank spectra and perturbation, and both
  exact integer sign certificates with SymPy.
- `scripts/verify_cqc_numpy.py` separately recomputes the rank-two and
  full-rank certificates numerically with NumPy.
- `scripts/verify_cqc_mpmath.py` starts from separately entered
  physical-basis amplitudes and checks the certificate at 60 digits with
  mpmath. It additionally evaluates Iqbal's Proposition-3 sufficient
  condition, the Coles–Piani bound, and ECQC over all four qutrit MUBs.
- `notebooks/cqc_counterexample.nb` is a Mathematica walkthrough with an
  exact symbolic core (state, marginals, MUB checks, rational tables,
  entropy identities, and both integer sign certificates) plus a 40-digit
  Iqbal/Coles-Piani supplement. Expected outputs precede every input cell;
  all 19 cells were batch-evaluated in a fresh Wolfram 14.3 kernel with
  WolframScript 1.13.0.
